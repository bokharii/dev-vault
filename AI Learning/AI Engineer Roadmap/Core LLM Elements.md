* **Tokens** are the fundamental units of text that LLMs process, created by **breaking text into smaller components** such as words, subwords, or characters.
	* they are crucial because models predict the next token in sequences, API costs are based on token count, and models have maximum token limits for input and output
	* essentially tokens are the language and currency powering modern AI
	* AI tokens are tiny units of data that come from breaking down bigger chunks of information
	* the goal is to achieve the fastest processing time and lowest cost per token to optimize AI infrastructure and maximize revenue generation

* **Context** refers to the information provided to the model alongside the prompt, which it uses to generate a relevant and coherent response
	* essentially it's background knowledge that the LLM uses to guide its reasoning and generation process. for instance, **supporting text or previous turns in a conversation**.
	* the **context window** or context length of a LLM is the amount of text, in **tokens**, that the model can consider or "remember" at any one time
		* basically the LLM's working memory
		* the larger the context window, the more accurate its responses. however, requires a lot more computational power and thus costs


* **Sampling Parameters** are settings that control the randomness and creativity of the **text** generated by the LLM
	* ![[Screenshot 2026-07-15 at 12.52.16 PM.png]]
	* impacts how the model chooses the next word in the sequence
	* the sampling parameters can be adjusted for more **creative and imaginative** responses, or **predictable and safe** outputs
	* **Temperature** is a parameter that controls the **randomness** of the generated text.
		* higher temperature (1.0) leads to more diverse and unpredictable outputs, as the model is more likely to sample less probable words
		* lower temperature (0.2) results in more conservative and deterministic outputs, favoring the most likely words according to the models training data
		* basically **temperature influences the probability distribution from which the next word is selected**. higher temperature - model will use less probable words
	* **Top-K** sampling is a method used by LLMs during text generation to select the next word
		* instead of considering the entire vocabulary, it essentially uses a K value that narrows down the choices
		* low K values (1-10) produce conservative and factual outputs
		* medium (20-50) balance creativity and quality
		* high values (50+) result in highly creative word choices and outputs
		* use **high K for creative writing, low K for technical tasks like coding**
	* **Top-P** sampling AKA **nucleus sampling** is also a technique used to generate text. Its an alternative to Top K that dynamically adjusts the number of tokens considered based on their cumulative probability.
		* more adaptive than Top K, allows for flexible token selection. Low values (0.1-0.5) produce focused outputs, (0.6-0.9) balance creativity and coherence, and high (0.9-0.99) enable creative diversity
	* Basically temperature controls the **randomness** of the LLM's word choices. Top-k and Top-p are *filters* that further narrow the pool of eligible words before the AI makes its choice. Kind of like a safety net
		* set the **base** randomness using temperature
		* use Top-p or Top-k as **guardrails**
	* **Repetition Penalties** basically promote diversity in the vocabulary used in LLM's output
		* frequency penalty and presence penalty
		* improves overall output quality by **preventing redundant phrasing**
