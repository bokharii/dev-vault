* **Tokens** are the fundamental units of text that LLMs process, created by **breaking text into smaller components** such as words, subwords, or characters.
	* they are crucial because models predict the next token in sequences, API costs are based on token count, and models have maximum token limits for input and output
	* essentially tokens are the language and currency powering modern AI
	* AI tokens are tiny units of data that come from breaking down bigger chunks of information
	* the goal is to achieve the fastest processing time and lowest cost per token to optimize AI infrastructure and maximize revenue generation

* **Context** refers to the information provided to the model alongside the prompt, which it uses to generate a relevant and coherent response
	* essentially it's background knowledge that the LLM uses to guide its reasoning and generation process. for instance, **supporting text or previous turns in a conversation**.
	* the **context window** or context length of a LLM is the amount of text, in **tokens**, that the model can consider or "remember" at any one time
		* basically the LLM's working memory
		* the larger the context window, the more accurate its responses. however, requires a lot more computational power and thus costs
